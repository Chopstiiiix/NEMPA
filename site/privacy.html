Sparrowtell Privacy Policy

Last updated 22 July 2026

Sparrowtell is a community safety app for missing-person and robbery alerts in Nigeria. It broadcasts alerts to people near an incident and lets you raise an emergency SOS. This policy explains what we collect, why, and who can see it.

Sparrowtell is not affiliated with the US AMBER Alert system or with any government emergency service. It is a community tool. In a life-threatening emergency, contact your local emergency services first.

When recording happens

Audio is only recorded while an SOS or danger alert that you triggered is active. It starts when you raise the alert and stops when you end it. Sparrowtell does not record at any other time, and it cannot be started remotely by anyone else.

Your live location trail runs on the same basis: only while your own alert is active.

What becomes public

Nothing you file is public automatically. A report stays private to you until one of our operators reviews it and decides to broadcast it. If they do, the alert's title, description, photo, incident location and the details of the missing person become visible in the public feed and are pushed to phones within about 25km.

Your phone number, home address, National Identification Number and SOS audio are never broadcast and never public.

Information about other people

A missing-person report is, by nature, information about someone else — often a child. Only file a report about a person if you are entitled to share their details for the purpose of finding them. We ask operators to review every report before broadcast, and we will take an alert down quickly on request from the person concerned or their family. If an alert has already been sent, taking it down also sends a retraction to the phones that received it.

Emergency contacts you save are other people's details too. Please only add someone who would expect to be contacted on your behalf in an emergency.

Who we share it with

We do not sell your data, use it for advertising, or track you across other apps and websites. We share it only with the services that run Sparrowtell:

  • Supabase — database, accounts and file storage
  • Google Firebase Cloud Messaging — delivering push notifications
  • Resend and Amazon SES — sending account emails
  • Cloudflare — hosting this page and our domain
  • Gecko Intel — the operator console our own team uses to review reports and respond to SOS events

We may also disclose information where we are legally required to, or where it is necessary to protect someone's life.

How long we keep it

  • Account and profile data — until you delete your account
  • SOS events, location trails and audio — kept as an incident record; deleted with your account
  • Alerts that were broadcast — kept as a public safety record. If you delete your account, your name is detached from them but the alert itself may remain

Deleting your account

Open Sparrowtell, go to Account, scroll to Danger zone and choose Delete account. This permanently removes your profile, emergency contacts, registered devices, SOS history and any audio recorded during your alerts. It cannot be undone.

Your choices

  • Location, microphone and notification permissions can be turned off at any time in your phone's Settings. Turning off location or the microphone will stop the matching part of an SOS from working.
  • You can delete individual emergency contacts from the Account screen.
  • You can ask us to correct or delete information by emailing us.

Children

Sparrowtell is intended for adults. We do not knowingly create accounts for children under 13. Reports about children are a core purpose of the app, and are handled as described above.

Security

Data is transmitted over HTTPS and stored with per-user access rules, so one account cannot read another's private information. SOS audio is stored in a private bucket accessible only to you and our operators. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
